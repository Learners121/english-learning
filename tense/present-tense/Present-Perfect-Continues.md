# Present Perfect Continuous tense:- 

## Defination- 
> **Present Perfect Continuous Tense is a tense which is used to indicate an action that began in the past and has lasted or continued until the present moment. It uses the auxiliary verb i.e. has been or have been + present participle. Some examples of present perfect continuous tense are â€“ I have been waiting here for three hours, He has been singing in the band for two years, Nancy has been teaching at the school since January. Thus, you use the present perfect continuous tense while describing any past event or condition which is continuing in the present moment.**

# Positive Structure: 
#### ```Subject + am/is/are + present participle (verb+ing) + the rest of the sentence```

#### Positive Examples :-
 
1. He is learning French.
2. The girls are singing now.
3. I am opening my shop at the moment.
4. They are driving fast now.
5. They are playing with her son now.
6. I am writing again this code.
7. They are saying to your friend. 
8. We are playing.
9. You are going to the home.
10. He is coming my house.
---
# Negative Structure:
#### ``` Subject + am/is/are + not + present participle (verb+ing) + the rest of the sentence```

####  Negative  Examples :-
 
1. He is not learning French.
2. The girls are not singing now.
3. I am not opening my shop at the moment.
4. They aren't driving fast now.
5. They aren't playing with her son now.
6. I am not writing again this code.
7. They aren't saying to your friend. 
8. We aren't playing.
9. You aren't going to the home.
10. He is not coming my house.
---

 # Interrogative Structure: 
 #### ```Am/is/are + subject + present participle (verb+ing) + the rest of the sentence```

#### Interrogative Examples :-
 
1. Is He learning French.
2. Are The girls singing now.
3. Am I opening my shop at the moment.
4. Are They driving fast now.
5. Are They playing with her son now.
6. Am I not writing again this code.
7. Are They saying to your friend. 
8. Are We playing.
9. Are You going to the home.
10. Is He not coming my house.
---
# WH Words:- `Whose, Where, Who, Why, How, What, Whom, When, Which.`
# Wh Structure: 
#### ```Wh + Subject + am/is/are + present participle (verb+ing) + the rest of the sentence```
1. Why He is learning French.
2. Which The girl is singing now.
3. How I am opening my shop at the moment.
4. Whose They are calling fist now.
5. Whom They are playing with her son now.
6. Why I am writing again this code.
7. When They are saying to your friend. 
8. Where We are playing.
9. What You are going to the home.
10. Why He is coming my house.
---
# Wh Negative Interrogative Structure: 
#### ```Wh + Subject + am/is/are + not + present participle (verb+ing) + the rest of the sentence```
1. Why He is not learning French.
2. Which The girl is not singing now.
3. How I am not opening my shop at the moment.
4. Whose They aren't calling fist now.
5. Whom They aren't playing with her son now.
6. Why I am not writing again this code.
7. When They aren't saying to your friend. 
8. Where We aren't playing.
9. What You are not going to the home.
10. Why He is not coming my house.
---
#### Explanation : 
In the above sentences, the actions are taking place at the moment. It means that the present continuous tense shows an ongoing action in the present.
